Getting Your License in India (2025)

In India, the Amateur Station Operator's Certificate (ASOC) is conducted by the Wireless Planning and Coordination (WPC) Wing. You need to pass this exam to legally operate.

1 Choose Your Grade

Best for Beginners. Allows you to use VHF/UHF for local communication and limited HF power.

  • ✓ No Morse Code test required.
  • ✓ Max Power: 10 Watts (HF), 25 Watts (VHF).
  • ✓ Voice mode allowed on all allotted bands.

For Advanced Ops. Allows higher power and more frequency privileges.

  • ⚠ Morse Code (receiving/sending) @ 8 wpm is traditionally required (Check latest WPC circulars as rules evolve).
  • ✓ Max Power: 400 Watts.
  • ✓ Full access to satellite & experimental modes.

2 The Application Roadmap

Register on Saral Sanchar

Go to the DoT's Saral Sanchar Portal. Create a profile. This is the only official way to apply.

Apply for ASOC Exam

Select "Amateur Radio Exam". Choose your nearest monitoring station center. Pay the nominal fee (approx ₹100 via BharatKosh).

Study & Pass

Study electronics basics (Resistors, Ohm's law) and Regulations (Callsign blocks, Q-codes). The pass mark is usually 40-50%.

Wait for License

After passing, your result is processed. You then pay the license fee (20 years or lifetime). Download your digital license!

Pro Tip

"Join a local Ham Radio club (like IIH or ARC in your city). They often conduct classes and help with the Saral Sanchar paperwork!"
